# **About**
Drug–target interaction (DTI) prediction is a fundamental task in drug discovery, aiming to computationally determine whether a given drug molecule will bind to a specific protein target, and to quantify the strength of that interaction, known as binding affinity. Accurate DTI prediction enables researchers to prioritize the most promising drug candidates, reducing the time, cost, and experimental effort required in the drug discovery process. Recent advances in deep learning have demonstrated powerful capabilities for DTI prediction. Typically, these models take as input a protein sequence, represented as an amino acid string, and a drug molecule, represented as a SMILES string, and output a predicted binding affinity often as some scalar score value. Despite their success, most existing models overlook critical chemical features such as stereochemistry and molecular descriptors, which capture 3D spatial arrangement, chirality, and other chemical properties that strongly influence binding outcomes. To address these limitations, we propose FusionDTA: a multiview deep learning framework that incorporates multiple input branches (views): protein sequences, ligand SMILES sequences, stereochemistry tokens extracted from SMILES, and molecular descriptors. Each input is encoded using specialized neural network modules, and the resulting feature vectors are then combined to predict binding affinity. By explicitly including stereochemistry and chemical descriptors, our model aims to capture richer chemical and biophysical information, improving predictive accuracy and generalization in drug discovery tasks.
